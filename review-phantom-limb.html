Phantom Limb

VASKI

By Oren Atias · February 6, 2026 · Warp Records

8.8
Best New Music

VASKI's second album arrives with the weight of expectation that follows a perfect debut. 2023's Nerve Ending was a revelation — club music that felt like chamber music, beats that breathed. Phantom Limb doesn't try to repeat that trick. Instead, the Finnish producer goes somewhere stranger, building an album about absence that is paradoxically one of the most present, physical electronic records in years.

The concept — music about things that aren't there — manifests in the production. Kick drums are felt more than heard, occupying a sub-bass frequency that vibrates your sternum. Melodies start and stop mid-phrase, like thoughts interrupted. "Residual" opens with a synth line that ascends toward a resolution it never reaches, looping back on itself for four minutes until you realize the loop is the point — the ache of almost-arrival.

"Referred Pain" is the album's centerpiece and its most accessible track. Built around a chopped vocal sample that might be singing "stay" or might be singing "away" — the ambiguity is clearly intentional — it's a dance track in the truest sense, music designed to move a body through space. At 128 BPM it sits in the sweet spot between trance and house, borrowing the emotional weight of the former and the physicality of the latter.

The closing stretch — "Proprioception," "Where the Limb Was," "Still Reaching" — is extraordinary. Three tracks that function as a single twenty-minute suite, gradually stripping away elements until the final track is just a pulse, a single rhythmic element that fades into silence so slowly you're not sure when the music ends and the room begins.

VASKI has made an album about loss that never once feels sad. Phantom Limb is about the strange beauty of absence — the way a missing thing can be more vivid than something present. It's electronic music at its most human.
